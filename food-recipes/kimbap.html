Kimbap (김밥) recipe

Ingredients

  • 5 sheets of kim (seaweed paper) (sometimes called “nori” from Japanese)
  • 4 cups cooked rice
  • 1 large carrot, cut into matchsticks
  • 1 cucumber, cut into matchsticks
  • 5 strips of yellow pickled radish
  • 1/2 cup of tuna
  • 3 eggs
  • soft cheese (Almette)
  • soy sauce
  • young ginger
  • sesame
  • sesame oil
  • mayonnaise
  • pepper

Directions

  1. Place freshly made rice in a large, shallow bowl. Gently mix in 2 teaspoons toasted sesame oil and 4 spoons of sesame over top with wooden spoon. Set it aside so it can cool down.
  2. Mix tuna with 1 spoon of mayonnaise and pinch of pepper.
  3. Crack 3 eggs in a bowl and add ¼ teaspoon salt. Beat it with fork and remove the stringy chalaza.
  4. Drizzle a few drops of oil on a heated 10 to 12 inch non-stick pan. Wipe off the excess with a paper towel so only a thin sheen of oil remains. Turn down the heat to low and pour the egg mixture into the pan. Spread it into a large circle so it fills the pan.
  5. When the bottom of the egg is cooked, flip it over with a spatula. Remove from the heat and let it cook slowly in the hot pan for about 5 minutes, with the ultimate goal of keeping the egg as yellow as possible, and not brown.
  6. Cut it into ½ inch wide strips. Put it next to the spinach on the platter.
  7. Place a sheet of kim on a bamboo mat with the shiny side down. Evenly spread about ¾ cup of cooked rice over top of it, leaving about 2 inches uncovered on one side of the kim.
  8. Place tuna, carrot, cucumber, yellow pickled radish strip, a few egg strips, and cheese in the center of the rice.
  9. Use both hands to roll the mat (along with kim and rice) over the fillings, so one edge of the rice and kim reaches the opposite edge. This centers the fillings in the roll, so they’ll be nicely in the middle when you slice it.
  10. Grab the mat with both hands and and press it tightly as you continue rolling the kimbap. Push out the mat as you roll, so it doesn’t get wrapped in the kimbap.
  11. Remove the roll from the mat at the end and set the finished roll aside with the seam down, to seal it nicely.
  12. Repeat 4 more times with the remaining ingredients.
  13. Put some toasted sesame oil on the finshed rolls and sprinkle some sesame seeds over top. Cut each roll into ¼ inch bite size pieces with a sharp knife, occasionally wiping it with a wet paper towel or cloth to clean the starch off and to ease cutting.
  14. Put it on a plate and serve immediately or pack it in a lunchbox. Eat dipping each roll in the soy sauce. Between pieces try eating some young ginger strips.

Enjoy!

Steamed bun - baozi (包子) recipe

Ingredients

  • 300g all-purpose flour
  • 3g salt
  • 150-170ml water (adjust the softness)
  • 1 and ½ teaspoon active instant yeast
  • 3 cups finely chopped Chinese chive
  • 2 tablespoons sesame oil (divided)
  • 30g bean vermicelli (soak in clean water until soft)
  • pinch of salt
  • ¼ teaspoon Chinese five spice powder

Directions

  1. In a large bowl or in a stand-mixer bowl, add flour, instead yeast, water and salt.
  2. Grasp all the ingredients together and then knead the flour into smooth and soft dough. At the very beginning, it might be a little bit sticky. Just keep kneading and you will get cute dough (or you can simply use a stand mixer to knead the dough for 7-8 minutes with slow speed).
  3. Cover the bowl and let the dough rest for around 1 hour (the time might be longer in winter )or until the paste ball doubles in size.
  4. Mix the chives with 2 teaspoons of sesame oil. You will end up with juicy and green chive after steaming.
  5. Add some vegetable oil to a pan and make a egg omelet. Cool for several minutes and cut into small pieces.
  6. Completely drain soaked bean bean vermicelli and then cut into shorter pieces. Transfer them to the filling bowl, and add salt, sesame oil and Chinese five spice powder. Give a big stir fry to mix everything well.
  7. When the dough is ready, transfer the dough to a slightly floured board and then knead forcefully until a smooth surface. Then divide the dough into 12 portions. Roll each portion into a circle.
  8. Place 1 tablespoon of filling in the middle. Wrap it.
  9. Place them in steamer, turn up the fire and heat for 2-3 minutes, turn off the fire and wait for 10 to 15 minutes. Then start the fire and steam the buns for 10 minutes after the water boils. Wait for 5 minutes with lid covered.

Enjoy!
